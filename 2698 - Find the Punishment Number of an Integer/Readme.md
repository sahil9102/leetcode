Find the Punishment Number of an Integer</b><b></b></p><p>Solved</p><p>Medium</p><p>Topics</p><p>Companies</p><p>Hint</p><p>Given a positive integer n, return the <i>punishment number</i> of n.</p><p>The punishment number of n is defined as the sum of the squares of all integers i such that:</p><p>1 <= i <= n</p><p>The decimal representation of i * i can be partitioned into contiguous substrings such that the sum of the integer values of these substrings equals i.</p><p> </p><p>Example 1:</p><p>Input: n = 10</p><p>Output: 182</p><p>Explanation: There are exactly 3 integers i in the range [1, 10] that satisfy the conditions in the statement:</p><p>- 1 since 1 * 1 = 1</p><p>- 9 since 9 * 9 = 81 and 81 can be partitioned into 8 and 1 with a sum equal to 8 + 1 == 9.</p><p>- 10 since 10 * 10 = 100 and 100 can be partitioned into 10 and 0 with a sum equal to 10 + 0 == 10.</p><p>Hence, the punishment number of 10 is 1 + 81 + 100 = 182</p><p>Example 2:</p><p>Input: n = 37</p><p>Output: 1478</p><p>Explanation: There are exactly 4 integers i in the range [1, 37] that satisfy the conditions in the statement:</p><p>- 1 since 1 * 1 = 1. </p><p>- 9 since 9 * 9 = 81 and 81 can be partitioned into 8 + 1. </p><p>- 10 since 10 * 10 = 100 and 100 can be partitioned into 10 + 0. </p><p>- 36 since 36 * 36 = 1296 and 1296 can be partitioned into 1 + 29 + 6.</p><p>Hence, the punishment number of 37 is 1 + 81 + 100 + 1296 = 1478</p><p> </p><p>Constraints:</p><p>1 <= n <= 1000</p><p></p>
